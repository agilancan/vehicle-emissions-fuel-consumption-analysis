# Vehicle Emissions & Fuel Consumption Analysis (Canada 2015–2019)

Interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing the relationship between vehicle fuel consumption, CO₂ emissions, manufacturer trends, fuel types, and vehicle classes using Canadian government open data.

Built to explore how vehicle specifications and fuel efficiency correlate with environmental impact through interactive visual analytics and dashboard storytelling.

## Project Overview

This project investigates trends and correlations within Canada's vehicle fuel consumption and emissions dataset (2015–2019).

Using Tableau, I designed an interactive dashboard that allows users to explore:

- Overall emissions and fuel consumption trends over time
- Vehicle manufacturers contributing most to emissions
- Fuel type impact on overall emissions
- Vehicle class differences in fuel efficiency and CO₂ output
- Correlations between fuel consumption and emissions across multiple dimensions

The dashboard combines exploratory analysis, visual storytelling, and interactive filtering to surface insights around vehicle efficiency and environmental performance.

## Dataset

**Source:** Government of Canada Open Data – Fuel Consumption Ratings

## Key Insights

### 📉 Declining Trend in Emissions & Fuel Consumption

Analysis of 2015–2019 vehicle data showed a gradual decline in both fuel consumption and emissions over time, suggesting improvements in vehicle efficiency.

### 🚗 Manufacturer Impact

Manufacturers such as **Ford, Chevrolet, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, and Porsche** ranked among the largest contributors to emissions and fuel consumption.

Interestingly, **Toyota ranked lower than expected despite strong sales volume**, suggesting comparatively better fuel efficiency.

### ⛽ Fuel Type Contribution

Gasoline vehicles (regular + premium gasoline) accounted for the overwhelming majority of total emissions.

### SUV / Large Vehicle Effect

Larger vehicles — including **SUVs, sports cars, and pickup trucks** — consistently demonstrated higher emissions profiles than smaller vehicle classes.

### Strong Fuel ↔ Emissions Correlation

Multiple visual analyses confirmed a strong positive relationship between:

- Higher fuel consumption
- Increased CO₂ emissions

City driving also showed higher emissions patterns compared to highway driving.
